ALASKAN WAY

VIADUCT

The Alaskan Way Viaduct was an elevated freeway in Downtown Seattle. While it transported many people through each day, there were many long standing issues with the viaduct.

It was incredibly noisy due to tens of thousands of cars crossing daily. It was very congested, often having slow traffic, and it blocked off access to the waterfront for decades, which limited pedestrian access and economic growth.

The viaduct was removed and replaced by a tunnel in November of 2019. Its demolition allowed more space to improve the waterfront, which were then dedicated towards a new park, walkways, and bike paths.

As a result, mobility and quality of life has improved in Downtown seattle, thanks to the creation of the tunnel and new developments after the viaduct removal.

USA'S EXCESSIVE

DEPENDENCE ON CARS

While the automobile has grown to be an American staple over the last century, there are many issues that came along with it. American society has grown extremely dependent on cars, and its cities have been designed and built around cars, making it nearly impossible to thrive in modern America without a motor vehicle.

Attempting to sustain such a car-centric society is extremely costly. It comes at the heavy price of public health, highly congested and ugly roads, huge wastes of land, environmental harm, and much more.
