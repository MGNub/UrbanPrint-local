Walkable cities are important

Health

Walkable cities boost health by encouraging walking, reducing heart disease and stress. Portland’s residents take 20% more steps, while nature walks cut stress hormones by 10%, per Stanford.

Environment

Walkability cuts car use, lowering 29% of U.S. emissions. Copenhagen’s 62% walk-bike rate reduces CO2 by 30%, improving air quality and sparing land, unlike sprawling Los Angeles.

Economy

Walkable cities increase retail sales by 20%, per New York data, reduce $100 billion in road costs, and raise property values 15-20%, as seen in Arlington, Virginia.

Our Mission

We aim to shed light on the car-centric flaws of modern cities, advocating for walkable urban spaces that enhance health, reduce emissions, and boost local economies. By raising awareness, we envision communities where pedestrians thrive, sidewalks replace sprawl, and sustainable design reclaims streets from cars, fostering a vibrant, equitable living for all.
